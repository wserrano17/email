I finished Babel by R. F. Kuang, A Model World by Michael Chabon, and read the Committed by Viet Thanh Nguyen. Olivia T. recomended me Babel I think basically a year ago. It's a fantasy novel set in Oxford in the 1800s about a translation institute that can perform magic and stuff through the power of language. I thought it was fun but I think she needs to take a peek at the Language Files or take Athena Kirk's writing systems class b.c. she really insults egyptian heiroglyphs and makes some other dubious claims about language. I also think that shes pretty heavy handed with the anti-colonial stance idk. A Model World was fun. I read Mysteries of Pgh by Chabon a while ago and have been looking for more Chabon to chomp on. Its a short story collection with the first half following dudes who are down on their luck in romance and the second-half is a series of vignettes from this one guys life from childhood to like high school i think. I liked it. Chabon is really good at writing 2000s guys. Idk how else to describe it but its like the whole vibe of being a dude in the 90s-2000s and sad or something. Its like Coldplay + Scoliosis + Spring Awakening. Onto the Comitted by Viet Thanh Nguyen. Its the second book in a series? duo? after the Sympathizer. Its all about the Vietnamese refugee experience, and I really liked the first one. I liked the beginning and end of this one, but the middle was just too much. Comming off of Babel I didn't need to hear any more anything about colonialism. Also some of it just felt like facebook cringe. Like Aisan squatting and taking shoes off in the house are major plot points. Idk it just didn't hit like the first one.
